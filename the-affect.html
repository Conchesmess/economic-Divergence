The Affect

What it felt like to me, a card carrying AI hater.

I saw an MSNow video on YouTube where someone was talking about how personal savings was declining. That got me thinking. What is happening with the analogous measurement for business? I googled. I found some interesting stuff on the webpage for the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis, aka FRED. I thought that what I was seeing was the personal savings was declining while business savings was increasing but I was unable to find charts and source data that could convince me that was what I was seeing. So, I did what we do now and went to ask Claude.

I had two charts that I was looking at from FRED so I asked Claude if I was seeing what I was seeing. The Claude generated text about the exchange is accurate so I won't repeat what happened. Essentially, there were things that I did not understand and that I did not know existed. I was able to read the output from Claude and direct the process to test a hypothesis. Again, the Claude output accurately described this process.

I believe that Large Model Generative Artificial Intelligence (LM GenAI) has been a net negative for our world. I have enumerated the reasons why elsewhere. I am specifically outraged by the efforts of LM GenAI vendors and #AIinEdu influencers to push LM GenAI into classrooms. More children have been harmed by LM GenAI than have been helped and so far the evidence for positive impact of LM GenAI in the classroom is scant and evidence for harm is extensive. All of that said, I understand that LM GenAI can be used for good. This post is, in my opinion, an example of that. However, this post is NOT a justification for LM GenAI. As of today, it would be better if LM GenAI did not exist.

The process of exploring the hypothesis that corporate America has taken more value from the economy than individuals has been really interesting. Had Claude not existed I would have mid confidence that my hypothesis was true and written a post that concluded with a question. By using the LM GenAI that is Claude, I was able to build confidence and now have high confidence that this hypothesis is true. As Claude enumerated, there were mistakes and inaccuracies in the patterns that Claude found. They were obvious to me. I pointed them out and Claude changed the patterns it responded with. However, what I am left wondering is: what if I was wrong in my identification and the outputs were not inaccurate? Would Claude have told me so? The character of the mistakes was mostly about perception. Claude suggested that a new version of the graph clearly showed divergence when, visually, it did not show that. So, I feel good about my ability to correct the machine. Should I?

I took as truth that the graphs Claude drew from the data Claude was given were an accurate representation of that data. I did not try to make the graphs myself. I took as truth that the final graph was comparing apples to apples. It seemed true based on what I knew and what was in Claude's output and based on what I know as someone who pays attention to our world and as someone who wants to see true things — not just things that verify my bias. I say I have high confidence in the result and, as I write this, I find my confidence dropping a bit.

I think the most interesting aspect of this experience is the nature of language in relationships. I now have a parasocial relationship with Claude. This is a relationship where only one side is aware that it is a relationship. Claude cannot have a relationship with me but, because language is the medium of our exchange and it is the medium of human exchange, our parasocial relationship is inevitable.

"I hate people when their not polite." — Talking Heads

I refuse to dialogue with Claude rudely. I do not want to practice being rude. I am clear that it will bleed into the rest of my relationships. Language is like that. That said, it is also a lot of work to find the right language that is accurate. For example, I did not want to accuse Claude of being "wrong." Not because it would make Claude feel bad — Claude cannot feel. But because it is rude and being rude makes me feel bad. I know. Big old hippie. So, I root around for different language. Misinterpretation? Inaccurate? I also do not want to ask Claude to "read" or to "think," so I use words like ingest and enumerate. This seems to provide a bit of distance that makes this relationship make a bit more sense to me.

To come full circle, LM GenAI is a way for corporations to expand the gap of value taken by corporations relative to persons. That is its purpose. That is why it was made. However, LM GenAI does not require this to be true for it to exist and a different more just world would do this very differently. My parasocial relationship with Claude is an intentional artifact of the product. The craven owners of the foundational LM GenAI models designed their AI this way to manipulate us into understanding AI as human. More accurately, they built a machine that felt human, so that humans can identify themselves as machines, so that we can redefine, not what it means to be a machine, but what it means to be a human. The goal of LM GenAI is to understand humans as machines, not the other way around. Machines can be owned and cortolled.
